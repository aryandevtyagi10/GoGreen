As an Environmetalist, I’ve always been fascinated by the way technology connects people and systems. I found myself drawn toward web development. The idea of creating interactive, user-friendly websites and applications became an exciting new challenge for me.
To expand my skill set, I started exploring full-stack web development. From learning front-end technologies like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to mastering back-end development with Node.js, Express, and databases like MongoDB, my journey has been both demanding and rewarding. These new skills have empowered me to build complete, functional web applications that bridge the gap between users and technology.During this journey, I had the opportunity to complete an internship, where I applied my skills in a professional setting.This experience helped me solidify my knowledge but also allowed me to contribute to real-world projects, gaining invaluable insights into the workflow of web development teams.

It also represents a milestone in my web development journey, combining my engineering background with my new web development expertise.

Congolian Forest

The Congolian Rainforest, located in Central Africa, is the second-largest tropical rainforest in the world after the Amazon. It spans several countries, including the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC), Congo, Gabon, Cameroon, and the Central African Republic,covering approximately 1.8 million square kilometers.The Sundaland Rainforest spans approximately 1.5 million square kilometers across Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, and Brunei.

Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon Rainforest, often called the "Lungs of the Earth," is a vast and diverse geographical wonder. It spans across nine countries in South America, with the largest portion in Brazil. Covering approximately 5.5 million square kilometers, it is the largest tropical rainforest in the world.The region houses over 400 billion trees and is home to 10% of the world's known species, including unique flora and fauna.

Sundaland Rainforest

The Sundaland Rainforest, located in Southeast Asia, is one of the world’s most biodiverse and ecologically significant regions. It encompasses parts of the Malay Peninsula, Borneo, Sumatra, Java, and surrounding smaller islands.
